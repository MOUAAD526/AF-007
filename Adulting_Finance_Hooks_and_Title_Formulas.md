## SECTION 1 — THE ADULTING FINANCE TITLE FORMULA

Every title must pass through this 4-part filter before it qualifies.

```
[TRIGGER] + [SPECIFIC OUTCOME] + [ELI12 RELATABILITY] + [URGENCY / STAKES]
```

### Part 1 — TRIGGER (Makes the reader feel seen)
Choose one type:
- A number that promises structure → `"5 Ways," "$847," "3 Months"`
- A relatable scenario → `"When Your Rent Goes Up," "Before You Quit Your Job"`
- A pain point they already feel → `"Why You're Broke," "Your Credit Score Dropped"`
- A first-gen flag → `"Nobody Told Me," "What Schools Never Teach"`
- A curiosity gap → `"The Real Reason," "What Actually Happens"`

### Part 2 — SPECIFIC OUTCOME (The promise — must be concrete)
❌ "How to Save Money"
✅ "How to Save $1,000 in 90 Days on a $40K Salary"

The outcome must be specific enough that the reader can visualize the result before clicking.

### Part 3 — ELI12 RELATABILITY (The Adulting Finance fingerprint)
- Conversational language → `"Here's What Actually Happens"`
- Scenario-based setups → `"If You Have $500 in Savings and $8K in Debt, Read This"`
- Anti-jargon defaults → `"Credit Utilization" → "How Much of Your Credit Card You're Using"`
- Pop-culture adjacent framing without trend-chasing

### Part 4 — URGENCY / STAKES (Why now, why you — no fake scarcity)
Connect the topic to real consequences in the reader's life today.
The Money Dominoes logic lives here — hint at what falls next if they don't act.

### Full Formula Applied:
```
TRIGGER:          "3 Things That Happen"
SPECIFIC OUTCOME: "to Your Credit Score"
ELI12:            "When You Carry a Balance Every Month"
STAKES:           (implied consequence chain is the urgency)

FINAL TITLE: "3 Things That Happen to Your Credit Score When You Carry a Balance Every Month"
```

## SECTION 2 — THE TWO-TITLE STRATEGY

Every Adulting Finance post needs TWO versions of its title.

### SEO Title Tag — 40–60 Characters
- Appears in Google search results
- Lead with primary keyword or strongest hook
- Cut all filler words
- Use a bracket or number if it fits
- Never sacrifice clarity for cleverness

### H1 (On-Page Title) — 60–80+ Characters
- Appears on the page itself
- Full ELI12 voice — add the scenario, qualifier, empathy signal
- Google uses this as its rewrite source 50.76% of the time

### Workflow:
1. Write the H1 first — full voice, no limit
2. Compress to 40–60 chars for the SEO tag, preserve keyword + core promise
3. Check both against the Title Scorecard

### Example:
```
Primary Keyword: "zero based budget"

SEO Tag (42 chars): How to Zero-Base Your Budget in a Weekend
H1 (71 chars):      How to Zero-Base Your Budget in One Weekend — Even If Math Makes You Sweat
```
